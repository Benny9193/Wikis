Lore & History

The Elder Scrolls universe contains thousands of years of history, mythology, and deep lore. This page covers the essential background knowledge for understanding Skyrim's world, from the creation of Mundus to the current civil war.

üåü The Creation of Mundus

In the beginning, there was only the Void. From it emerged two primordial forces: Anu (stasis, order) and Padomay (change, chaos). Their interplay created Aurbis, the universe.

"Before the ages of man came the Dawn Era, when the Aedra and Daedra came to be."

From their conflict emerged:

  • Anuiel - The soul of Anu, representing order
  • Sithis - The soul of Padomay, representing chaos and the Void
  • Et'Ada - The Original Spirits, who would become the Aedra and Daedra

Lorkhan (called Shor by the Nords) convinced some Et'Ada to create Mundus, the mortal plane. Those who sacrificed their power to create it became the Aedra ("Our Ancestors"). Those who refused became the Daedra ("Not Our Ancestors").

The Eight Divines (Aedra)

The Eight Divines are the Aedra who gave their power to create Mundus. Talos, a mortal who achieved apotheosis, was added as the Ninth Divine but his worship is now banned by the White-Gold Concordat.

Akatosh

Dragon God of Time

Chief deity of the Divines. Father of dragons. Created the barrier between Mundus and Oblivion. Blessed St. Alessia with the Amulet of Kings.

Arkay

God of Life and Death

Governs the cycle of birth and death. His blessing protects corpses from necromancy. Priests of Arkay tend to the dead.

Dibella

Goddess of Beauty and Love

Patron of artists and lovers. Her temples can be found throughout Skyrim, including a prominent one in Markarth.

Julianos

God of Wisdom and Logic

Patron of scholars and mages. Associated with literature, law, and contradiction. Revered at the College of Winterhold.

Kynareth

Goddess of Air and Nature

Also called Kyne by the Nords. Goddess of the sky, wind, and elements. Taught mortals the Thu'um. Major temple in Whiterun.

Mara

Goddess of Love and Compassion

Mother goddess of Nirn. Patron of marriage (Amulet of Mara). Her temple in Riften performs wedding ceremonies.

Stendarr

God of Mercy and Justice

Patron of righteous might. The Vigilants of Stendarr hunt Daedra worshippers, vampires, and werewolves in his name.

Zenithar

God of Work and Commerce

Patron of merchants and craftsmen. Associated with honest trade and labor. His blessing aids smithing.

‚öîÔ∏è Talos - The Ninth Divine

Tiber Septim, born Hjalti Early-Beard (possibly also Zurin Arctus), was a mortal who united Tamriel and achieved apotheosis, becoming the god Talos. His worship was banned by the White-Gold Concordat to appease the Aldmeri Dominion, sparking the Skyrim Civil War.

The Thalmor believe Talos worship weakens the barrier between Mundus and Aetherius, threatening to unmake reality. Whether this is true or merely justification for persecution remains debated.

üêâ Dragons and the Dragon Cult

Dragons are immortal children of Akatosh, created in the Dawn Era. They ruled over mortals through the Dragon Cult in ancient Skyrim, with Dragon Priests serving as their intermediaries.

Alduin The World-Eater, firstborn of Akatosh. Destined to consume the world at the end of time. Not truly a god, but proclaimed himself one.
Paarthurnax Alduin's lieutenant who betrayed him. Taught mortals the Thu'um. Now meditates atop the Throat of the World.
Named Dragons Powerful unique dragons like Odahviing, Durnehviir, and Mirmulnir. Each has their own personality and history.
Dragon Priests Mortal servants granted power and immortality. Eight named priests guard Word Walls. Miraak was the first Dragonborn priest.

The Dragon War

When the dragons' rule became tyrannical, mortals rebelled in the Dragon War. Paarthurnax taught humans the Thu'um, allowing them to fight back. The ancient Nord heroes used the Elder Scroll to cast Alduin forward in time rather than kill him‚Äîleading directly to the events of Skyrim.

Historical Timeline

Dawn Era

Creation of Mundus

The Aedra sacrifice their power to create the mortal plane. Dragons and other beings come into existence.

Merethic Era

Elven Dominance

The Aldmer spread across Tamriel. Ysgramor leads the Five Hundred Companions to Skyrim. The Dragon Cult rises and falls.

First Era (1E 0 - 1E 2920)

The Alessian Empire

St. Alessia leads the slave rebellion against the Ayleids. The First Empire of Cyrodiil is established. Skyrim is united under High King Harald.

Second Era (2E 0 - 2E 896)

The Interregnum

The Akaviri Potentate rules, then falls. Tamriel fragments into warring states. Tiber Septim conquers all of Tamriel, ending the Era.

Third Era (3E 0 - 3E 433)

Septim Dynasty

The Septim Empire rules Tamriel. Ends with the Oblivion Crisis when Martin Septim sacrifices himself to stop Mehrunes Dagon.

Fourth Era (4E 0 - Present)

The Current Age

The Mede Dynasty takes power. The Great War with the Aldmeri Dominion. Skyrim begins in 4E 201, 200 years after the Oblivion Crisis.

‚öîÔ∏è The Great War (4E 171 - 4E 175)

The defining conflict that shaped the political landscape of Skyrim. The Aldmeri Dominion demanded the Empire ban Talos worship and cede territory. When Emperor Titus Mede II refused, war began.

The Empire

Cyrodiil, High Rock, and Skyrim (reluctantly)

VS

Aldmeri Dominion

Summerset Isles, Valenwood, and Elsweyr

Key Events:

  • 4E 174: The Imperial City falls to the Dominion
  • 4E 175: Battle of the Red Ring - Empire retakes the Imperial City
  • 4E 175: White-Gold Concordat signed - war ends in uneasy peace

The White-Gold Concordat:

  • Talos worship is banned throughout the Empire
  • The Blades are disbanded
  • Thalmor Justiciars may enforce the ban within Imperial territory
  • Hammerfell is released from the Empire (they refused to sign)

üõ°Ô∏è The Skyrim Civil War

The ongoing conflict when the game begins. Sparked when Jarl Ulfric Stormcloak killed High King Torygg using the Thu'um, claiming it was a legitimate challenge for the throne.

Imperial Legion

General Tullius, Jarls of Solitude, Markarth, Falkreath, Morthal

VS

Stormcloaks

Ulfric Stormcloak, Jarls of Windhelm, Riften, Dawnstar, Winterhold

The Stakes:

  • Stormcloak Victory: Skyrim gains independence, Talos worship is restored, but the Empire is weakened against the Dominion
  • Imperial Victory: Skyrim remains in the Empire, providing unity against the Thalmor, but Talos worship remains banned

Note: The player can join either faction and determine the outcome of the war.

Key Lore Concepts

The Thu'um (Voice)

The art of using dragon language to project power. Dragons use it naturally; mortals must spend years learning each Word. The Dragonborn can absorb dragon souls to learn instantly. The Greybeards dedicate their lives to its study.

Dragonborn (Dovahkiin)

Mortals born with the soul of a dragon, blessed by Akatosh. They can absorb dragon souls and learn Shouts instantly. Previous Dragonborn include Tiber Septim, the Septim bloodline, and Miraak (the first).

The Elder Scrolls

Artifacts of unknown origin that contain prophecy and infinite knowledge. Reading them causes blindness over time. The Moth Priests dedicate their lives to reading them. One was used to banish Alduin through time.

CHIM

A state of enlightenment where one realizes they exist within a dream (the Godhead's dream) yet maintains individual existence. Vivec and possibly Tiber Septim achieved CHIM. Most who attempt it zero-sum (cease to exist).

Mantling

"Walk like them until they walk like you." A method of achieving divinity by perfectly emulating a god until the universe can't tell the difference. The Champion of Cyrodiil mantled Sheogorath.

Towers

Metaphysical structures that anchor reality. The Adamantine Tower is the oldest. The Throat of the World may be Snow-Throat, Skyrim's tower. The Thalmor may seek to deactivate all towers to unmake reality.

Kalpas

Cycles of time. Alduin is meant to end each kalpa by eating the world, allowing a new one to begin. His desire to rule instead of destroy is why the current kalpa persists‚Äîand why he must be stopped.

Sovngarde

The Nordic afterlife, created by Shor (Lorkhan) for brave warriors. Located in Aetherius. Those who die valiantly in battle are taken there by Tsun. Alduin feeds on souls there during the events of Skyrim.
