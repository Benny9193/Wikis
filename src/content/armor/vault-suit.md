## Overview

The **Vault Suit** is the iconic blue and yellow jumpsuit issued to all residents of Vault-Tec vaults. While offering minimal protection, it has become a symbol of the Fallout series and the vault dweller's journey into the wasteland.

## Design

### Appearance
- Royal blue primary color
- Yellow/gold trim and accents
- Vault number displayed on the back
- Utility belt with pouches
- Durable, all-purpose material

### Features
- Temperature regulation
- Radiation-resistant lining (minimal)
- Multiple pockets for storage
- Flexible for ease of movement
- Unisex design

## History

Vault-Tec designed the Vault Suit as standard attire for all vault residents. The distinctive design served multiple purposes:
- Easy identification of vault residents
- Psychological uniformity and community
- Practical utility for vault life
- Brand recognition for Vault-Tec

## Variants

### Standard Vault Suits
Each vault has suits numbered accordingly:
- Vault 13 Suit (Fallout 1)
- Vault 101 Suit (Fallout 3)
- Vault 21 Suit (New Vegas)
- Vault 111 Suit (Fallout 4)
- Vault 76 Suit (Fallout 76)

### Modified Versions

#### Armored Vault Suit (Fallout 4)
- Ballistic weave can be applied
- Significantly improved protection
- Requires Railroad membership

#### Vault-Tec Lab Coat
- White coat variant for scientists
- Worn over the standard suit

#### Vault-Tec Security Armor
- Armored version for vault security
- Includes helmet and additional padding

## Stats

### Protection
- Base damage resistance: Very low
- No energy resistance (base)
- Minor radiation resistance in some versions

### Modding (Fallout 4)
With the right perks, Vault Suits can be modified:
- Ballistic weave (high DR/ER)
- Linings (various bonuses)
- Worn under armor pieces

## Cultural Impact

### In-Universe
- Instantly identifies someone as a vault dweller
- May provoke hostility (seen as naive outsiders)
- Or sympathy (recognized as survivors)
- Nostalgic value for some wastelanders

### Real World
- One of gaming's most recognizable outfits
- Popular cosplay choice
- Featured in merchandise and promotional material
- Symbol of the Fallout franchise

## Notable Wearers

- **The Vault Dweller** (Fallout 1)
- **The Chosen One** (Fallout 2)
- **The Lone Wanderer** (Fallout 3)
- **The Courier** (wore briefly, New Vegas)
- **The Sole Survivor** (Fallout 4)
- **Vault 76 Residents** (Fallout 76)

## Acquisition

Found in:
- Starting equipment (most games)
- Vault locations throughout the wasteland
- Abandoned Vault-Tec facilities
- Occasionally on vendors

## Trivia

- The number on the back helps NPCs identify your origin
- Some players keep the Vault Suit throughout the entire game
- Vault-Tec sold consumer versions before the war
- The blue and yellow color scheme was Vault-Tec's corporate colors
- Each vault's suits are slightly different in condition and fit
