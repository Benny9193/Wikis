## Overview

**Gun Nut** is an essential perk for any player focusing on ballistic weapons in Fallout 4 and Fallout 76. It unlocks the ability to craft and attach increasingly powerful modifications to guns at weapons workbenches.

## Effects

Each rank unlocks access to better weapon modifications:

| Rank | Level Required | Unlocks |
|------|----------------|---------|
| 1 | 1 | Basic mods (standard receivers, short barrels) |
| 2 | 13 | Improved mods (hardened receivers, better grips) |
| 3 | 25 | Advanced mods (powerful receivers, long barrels) |
| 4 | 39 | Expert mods (advanced receivers, best modifications) |

## Requirements

- **Intelligence 3** (base requirement)
- Specific level requirements for each rank
- Weapons workbench access
- Appropriate crafting materials

## Mod Categories Affected

### Receivers
- Damage output
- Fire rate
- Ammunition changes (automatic/semi-auto)

### Barrels
- Accuracy
- Range
- Recoil compensation

### Stocks/Grips
- Recoil management
- Hip-fire accuracy
- Aim-down-sights speed

### Magazines
- Ammunition capacity
- Reload speed

### Sights
- Accuracy
- Zoom levels
- Night vision capabilities

### Muzzles
- Suppressors
- Compensators
- Bayonets

## Strategic Importance

### Early Game
- Basic mods significantly improve starting weapons
- Can extend usefulness of common weapons
- Reduces need to find pre-modded weapons

### Mid Game
- Powerful receivers dramatically increase damage
- Suppressors enable stealth builds
- Magazine upgrades improve sustained combat

### Late Game
- Maximize weapon potential
- Create specialized loadouts
- Customize legendary weapons

Common materials needed:
- Adhesive
- Aluminum
- Screws
- Oil
- Steel
- Gears
- Springs

## Tips

1. **Prioritize early ranks** - The damage increase from better receivers is substantial
2. **Scrap weapons** - Mods can be learned by scrapping modded weapons
3. **Stock components** - Adhesive and screws are often bottlenecks
4. **Plan builds** - Know which mods you want before investing
