## Overview

**Fallout Tactics: Brotherhood of Steel** is a tactical role-playing game developed by Micro Forté and published by Interplay Entertainment in 2001. It shifts focus from traditional RPG gameplay to squad-based tactical combat while set in the Fallout universe.

## Setting

The game is set in the Midwest United States, primarily around the Chicago area, beginning in 2197. It follows a splinter faction of the Brotherhood of Steel that crashed in the region during an airship expedition.

## Plot

After their airships crash in the Midwest, the Brotherhood of Steel establishes a new chapter. Unlike their West Coast counterparts, this Midwestern Brotherhood begins recruiting from local populations out of necessity.

### Campaign Progression

The player leads a squad through increasingly difficult missions:
1. Establishing Brotherhood presence
2. Fighting raiders and tribal groups
3. Confronting super mutant remnants
4. Discovering and battling the Calculator

### The Calculator

The main antagonist is an AI called the Calculator, originally designed to rebuild civilization but gone rogue. It commands an army of robots and cybernetic creatures.

## Gameplay

### Squad-Based Tactics

- Control a squad of up to six characters
- Individual turn-based, continuous turn-based, or real-time modes
- Cover and positioning matter
- Line of sight mechanics

### Character System

- Uses modified SPECIAL system
- Multiple recruitable characters
- Different races available (humans, ghouls, super mutants, deathclaws)
- Extensive skill and perk system

### Mission Structure

- Linear campaign with discrete missions
- Optional side objectives
- Bunker bases between missions
- Vehicle missions available

## Brotherhood Differences

The Midwestern Brotherhood differs from other chapters:

- **Recruitment** - Accepts wastelanders, ghouls, super mutants, and even deathclaws
- **Philosophy** - More pragmatic, less isolationist
- **Structure** - More military-focused
- **Technology** - Uses what's available, not just pre-war tech

## Recruitable Races

### Humans
Standard recruits with balanced stats.

### Ghouls
Radiation resistant, often skilled.

### Super Mutants
High strength and endurance, limited intelligence options.

### Deathclaws
Powerful melee combatants, cannot use weapons or armor.

## Vehicles

The game features drivable vehicles:
- Hummer
- APC
- Tank
- Various others

Used in specific missions for transportation and combat.

## Major Locations

- **Bunker Alpha through Delta** - Brotherhood bases
- **Brahmin Wood** - Early tutorial area
- **Preoria** - Raider stronghold
- **Chicago** - Robot-controlled ruins
- **Vault 0** - Calculator's headquarters

## Multiplayer

Included multiplayer modes:
- Skirmish battles
- Assault missions
- CTF-style modes

## Canon Status

Fallout Tactics has semi-canon status:
- Major events are considered canon
- Specific details may be contradicted
- Referenced in later games
- Midwestern Brotherhood mentioned in Fallout 4

## Reception

The game received mixed reviews:
- Praised for tactical combat
- Criticized for departing from RPG roots
- Appreciated for expanding the universe
- Technical issues at launch

## Trivia

- Only Fallout game with real-time combat as default option
- Introduced drivable vehicles to the series
- First game to allow playing as non-human characters
- Features a multiplayer component
- The Midwestern Brotherhood is referenced in Fallout 3 and 4
