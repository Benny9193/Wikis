## Overview

**Fallout: Brotherhood of Steel** is an action role-playing game developed and published by Interplay Entertainment in 2004. It was the last Fallout game published by Interplay before the franchise was sold to Bethesda Softworks. The game departed significantly from traditional Fallout gameplay, adopting a console-focused action style.

## Setting

The game is set in the Carbon and Los areas of Texas, sometime after the events of Fallout 1. It follows initiates of the Brotherhood of Steel chapter operating in the region.

## Plot

The player is an initiate sent to find a missing Brotherhood patrol. The search leads to the town of Carbon, which has been overrun by raiders. The investigation uncovers a larger threat involving super mutants and a new strain of FEV.

### Story Progression

1. Investigate the missing patrol in Carbon
2. Discover the mutant threat
3. Travel to Los (Los Angeles ruins)
4. Confront the super mutant army
5. Defeat the Vault-Tec remnants behind the conspiracy

## Playable Characters

Three initiates are available:

### Cyrus
- Male human
- Balanced stats
- Standard Brotherhood initiate

### Cain
- Male ghoul
- Higher radiation resistance
- First playable ghoul protagonist

### Nadia
- Female human
- Higher agility
- Unlocked after completing the game once

## Gameplay

### Action-Focused

Unlike previous Fallout games:
- Real-time hack-and-slash combat
- No V.A.T.S. or turn-based elements
- Twin-stick shooter controls
- Linear level progression

### RPG Elements

Retained some RPG mechanics:
- Experience and leveling
- Skills to upgrade
- Weapon and armor selection
- Dialogue choices (limited)

### Cooperative Play

- Two-player local co-op
- Shared screen gameplay
- Both players progress together

## Weapons

The game features various weapons:
- Melee weapons (sledgehammers, blades)
- Small guns (pistols, SMGs)
- Big guns (miniguns, rocket launchers)
- Energy weapons (laser, plasma)
- Explosives

## Enemies

### Raiders
Generic human enemies in early game.

### Ghouls
Both feral and organized ghoul enemies.

### Super Mutants
The primary antagonist faction.

### Robots
Various automated threats.

### Radbugs/Creatures
Mutated wildlife.

## Canon Status

Fallout: Brotherhood of Steel is generally considered **non-canon**:
- Contradicts established lore
- Not referenced in later games
- Bethesda has distanced from it
- Often excluded from official timelines

### Lore Conflicts
- Depicts Brotherhood presence in Texas without explanation
- Timeline inconsistencies
- Tone conflicts with series style

## Development

### Troubled Production
- Interplay facing financial difficulties
- Meant to revitalize the franchise on consoles
- Bawls Guarana product placement controversy
- Licensed heavy metal soundtrack (Slipknot, etc.)

### Planned Sequel
A sequel, **Fallout: Brotherhood of Steel 2**, was in development but cancelled when Interplay lost the Fallout license.

## Reception

The game received mixed to negative reviews:
- Criticized for departing from Fallout's RPG roots
- Generic action gameplay
- Product placement seen as immersion-breaking
- Licensed soundtrack felt out of place
- Short campaign length

Considered one of the weakest entries in the franchise.

## Differences from Series

What sets it apart from other Fallout games:
- No SPECIAL character creation
- Linear progression (no open world)
- Action focus over RPG
- Licensed modern music
- In-game advertising
- Console exclusive
