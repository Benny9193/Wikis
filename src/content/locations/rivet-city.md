## Overview

**Rivet City** is the largest and most technologically advanced settlement in the Capital Wasteland, built within the beached remains of the aircraft carrier USS Rivet City. It represents the pinnacle of post-war civilization in the region.

## History

### The Aircraft Carrier

The USS Rivet City was a pre-war Nimitz-class aircraft carrier that became stranded in the Potomac River, likely during the chaos of the Great War. Over time, survivors discovered the vessel and began converting it into a permanent settlement.

### Foundation

The settlement was established decades before the events of Fallout 3. Early residents fortified the ship, converted its spaces into living quarters, and established the first stable government in the Capital Wasteland.

### Scientific Community

Rivet City became home to the wasteland's scientific elite. The establishment of the Science Lab attracted researchers who would eventually work on Project Purity.

## Project Purity Connection

Rivet City served as the base of operations for Dr. Li's scientific team:
- James (the player's father) worked here
- Plans for Project Purity were developed in the Science Lab
- The scientists eventually relocate to the Jefferson Memorial

## Economy

Rivet City thrives through:
- **Trade Hub** - Largest marketplace in the Capital Wasteland
- **Agriculture** - Hydroponic farming provides fresh food
- **Manufacturing** - Weapons and supplies production
- **Services** - Hotel, clinic, restaurants, and bars

## Security

The settlement maintains strong defenses:
- **Drawbridge** - Retractable entrance prevents unwanted access
- **Armed Guards** - Rivet City Security patrols constantly
- **Natural Defense** - The carrier's steel hull provides protection
- **Isolation** - Surrounded by water on most sides

## Cultural Significance

Rivet City represents:
- The possibility of rebuilding civilization
- Scientific advancement in the wasteland
- Functioning democracy and law
- The tension between the Commonwealth and Capital Wasteland
- A link between the old world (military vessel) and the new
